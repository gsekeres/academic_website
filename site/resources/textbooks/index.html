Recommendations

Textbooks

Books and lecture-note resources I have found useful in economics and mathematics.

Economics

Core Microeconomics

Use Mas-Colell, Whinston, and Green. If MWG is too complicated, use Varian, and then once you understand Varian, use MWG.

Game Theory

I got a lot from Osborne and Rubinstein.

Mechanism Design

Börgers is extremely good.

Econometrics

I use a combination of Hansen and Casella and Berger. For background, I also use the Blitzstein notes. I previously took Stefan Wager's course, and those notes are invaluable.

Macroeconomics

I mostly use Stokey, Lucas, and Prescott, along with Ljungqvist and Sargent. For coding, there is no better resource than QuantEcon.

Mathematics

Analysis

Johnsonbaugh and Pfaffenberger is, in my opinion, brilliant. By pacing, it assumes some prior knowledge, but otherwise is self-contained.

Linear Algebra

Strang. Also watch his lectures.

Probability

Joe Blitzstein's undergraduate and graduate notes are fabulous.

Convex Optimization

Boyd and Vandenberghe. If you are at Stanford, take the course with Boyd as soon as possible.
