# Source Topic Map

This map guides the curated bank. The raw extraction in `question_bank/` is treated as a source index only.

## Math for Economics

Primary local sources:

- ECON 6170 notes: real sequences, topology, convexity, correspondences, differentiation, static optimization, comparative statics, fixed points, duality.
- Simon and Blume, *Mathematics for Economists*: calculus, optimization, convexity, comparative statics.
- Johnsonbaugh and Pfaffenberger, *Foundations of Mathematical Analysis*: proof practice, sequences, compactness, continuity.
- Sydsaeter, Hammond, Seierstad, Strom, *Further Mathematics for Economic Analysis*: advanced optimization and dynamics; use selectively because OCR is noisy.

Target families:

- suprema, infima, monotone sequences, subsequences, limsup/liminf;
- open, closed, compact, continuous, uniformly continuous, semicontinuous;
- convex sets, convex hull, separation, convex/quasiconvex functions;
- correspondences, graph closedness, upper and lower hemicontinuity, Berge maximum theorem;
- unconstrained and constrained optimization, KKT, envelope theorem;
- comparative statics, implicit function theorem, single-crossing and increasing differences;
- Brouwer, Kakutani, and Tarski-style fixed point arguments;
- duality and Lagrangian weak/strong duality.

## Microeconomic Theory

Primary local sources:

- Varian, *Microeconomic Analysis*.
- Kreps, *Microeconomic Foundations*.
- Mas-Colell, Whinston, and Green, *Microeconomic Theory* OCR slices where usable.

Target families:

- preferences, utility representation, convexity, monotonicity;
- Marshallian demand, indirect utility, Walras' law, homogeneity;
- Hicksian demand, expenditure, Shephard's lemma, Slutsky decomposition;
- revealed preference, WARP/SARP/GARP, Afriat-style reasoning;
- producer theory, profit maximization, cost minimization, supply, conditional factor demand;
- partial and general equilibrium, welfare theorems, Edgeworth box;
- uncertainty, expected utility, risk aversion, insurance;
- information economics and adverse selection when a fully self-contained model can be written.

## Game Theory

Primary local sources:

- Osborne and Rubinstein, *A Course in Game Theory*.
- Osborne and Rubinstein solution manual as a private correctness/reference aid only.
- Fudenberg and Tirole OCR slices where usable.

Target families:

- strict and weak dominance;
- rationalizability and best-response beliefs;
- pure and mixed Nash equilibrium;
- zero-sum games and maxmin/minmax;
- extensive-form games, backward induction, subgame perfection;
- Bayesian games and Bayes-Nash equilibrium;
- repeated games and one-shot deviation logic;
- bargaining and Nash bargaining;
- implementation/mechanism design only when the model is fully stated.

## External Sources

Use external sources only to fill gaps after local sources. MIT OpenCourseWare problem sets are preferred when available, followed by other university problem sets. External prompts should be used for topic inspiration and citation anchors; write the final question and solution independently in the project schema.
